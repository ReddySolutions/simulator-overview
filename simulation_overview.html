How Calls Are Routed

Every inbound payment call is classified along four decision points: who is calling (Owner, Payer, or Non-Owner), how they were authenticated (IVR pre-auth, manual 3-question, 1-question, or none), whether authentication succeeded or failed, and what payment method they use (new credit card, new ACH e-check, or saved wallet). These decisions produce 10 sub-types and 66 total recipes shown below.

The Three Agent Systems

All 66 recipes use the same three applications. The simulation replicates each system faithfully so trainees practice in a realistic multi-app environment.

CustomerFirst (Salesforce Lightning CRM)

Account lookup, customer records, privacy verification panel, authentication scripts. This is the agent's primary workspace where they identify the caller, verify their relationship to the policy, and navigate authentication. The verification panel shows caller-type-specific question pools with a "DO NOT READ TO CALLER" disclaimer.

Paymentus Agent Dashboard

A 3-step payment wizard: account search, customer & payment information, and confirm payment. Includes credit card forms, E-Check forms, wallet selection, payment processing screens, and success confirmation pages. The agent configures policy rows, selects payment methods, collects card or bank details, and reads a verbatim authorization script before submitting.

CXone Softphone

Call controls, agent status indicator, call timer, and the PCI secure pause button. Appears as a compact right-side panel. The agent uses it to manage call state and, critically, to pause recording before collecting sensitive payment information.

Authentication Rules

Authentication requirements vary by the caller's relationship to the policy. These rules are compliance-critical and enforced in every simulation recipe.

Shared Call Flow Phases

The canonical payment call has 10 phases. Six are identical across all 66 happy-path recipes. The four variable phases have a small number of well-defined variants. Sub-Type 1j (Authentication Failure) terminates at Phase 3 and does not reach Phases 4–10.

Phase Steps Description Shared?
1. Greeting 1-3 Answer, customer states reason, assurance All 66
2. Identification 4-7 Account lookup, verify identity 4 variants
3. Authentication 8-10 Verify caller has rights 4 variants (incl. none) + fail outcome on 3 authenticating variants (1j)
4. Contact Info 9-12 Phone + email confirmation All 63 happy-path (N/A for 1j — call terminated)
5. Paymentus Setup 11-17 Open, search, select, configure All 63 happy-path (N/A for 1j)
6. Payment Collection 18-23 Card/bank/wallet entry 4 methods x new/wallet (N/A for 1j)
7. Auth Script 23-24 VERBATIM Required Call Component CC or E-Check (N/A for 1j)
8. Processing 24-27 Make Payment, wait, confirm All 63 happy-path (N/A for 1j)
9. Self-Service + Recurring 28 Education + recurring offer 3 variants (N/A for 1j)
10. Close 29-31 Additional help, goodbye All 63 happy-path + 1j uses a distinct Terminal Auth-Fail Close (3 recipes)

Note: Sub-Type 1j (Authentication Failure) terminates at Phase 3 before Paymentus is opened. It reuses Phases 1–2 from its parent path and adds a dedicated Terminal Close sub-flow (inform auth failed, offer alternatives, professional close). Phases 4–10 of the canonical happy-path flow do not apply.
